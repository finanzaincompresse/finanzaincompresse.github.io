mortgage

What is a mortgage?

The mortgage is a particular type of contract that involves two subjects: the lender, that is the person who disburses a sum of money or a quantity of material goods, and the borrower, who the person who undertakes to repay the agreed sum at the due date. A loan has reciprocal characteristics, which is that it is lent must then be repaid within a certain period of time.

Step

You go to a bank or a credit institution and apply for a mortgage.

Step

If the application is accepted, the loan is taken out.

Step

The money is transferred.

Step

Return of the amount granted, including interest, at the agreed term.

mortgage risks

What to be careful
about making a mortgage?

Taking out a mortgage is a very important decision.
It is a long-term commitment and that is why a great deal of attention must be paid to it. Even the Bank of Italy has made a number of points in its guide to mortgages.

Wise choice

You have to choose the most advantageous rate and opt for the duration that best suits your needs. Then it is important to compare several mortgages at different rates so that you can choose the most convenient one.

Cost verification

Check the APR (Annual Percentage Rate of Charge) or the SCI (Synthetic Cost Indicator): the former indicates the total cost of financing as a percentage, the latter does not take account of the costs for the valuer, the notary and non-compulsory policies.

Mortgage-to-property ratio

Pay attention to the relationship between the amount of the loan and the value of the property given as collateral. The value of the property is decided by an appraiser and is not what you paid to purchase it. The lower the ratio, the lower the interest rate. This is because the bank/loan institution that lends you the loan, if you can't pay them back, they can more easily cover the credit.

Document request

Request, if the bank has not delivered it, the PIES (European Standardised Prospectus). This allows a faster comparison of the mortgages offered by different banks. Also ask for the repayment plan.

Information

Inquire about the obligations of the credit intermediary if it is present.

Free to change

Keep in mind that the mortgage can be terminated in advance and that you can move it to another bank without charges or penalties.

rates

Fixed rate
&
variable rate

The interest rate represents the percentage of interest on a loan, i.e. the amount of remuneration payable to the lender and the cost to a borrower.

Fixed rate

PROS

  • 1Mortgage instalment and term remain constant over time
  • 2The repayment fee and the contract do not vary until the end
  • 3There is no change in the amount
  • 4Interest rates remain unchanged

Fixed rate

CONS

  • 1Constant interest rates are disadvantageous because the opportunities that arise from falling interest rates cannot be seized

Variable rate

PROS

  • 1In a first period the variable rate, compared to the fixed rate, is cheaper
  • 2If inflation decreases, the borrower benefits as the installment will decrease

Variable rate

CONS

  • 1If inflation increases, the instalment will also be affected and its amount will rise.
  • 2The value of the instalment, both positive and negative, will in any case depend on market fluctuations.

Green rate


Green mortgage is a soft loan designed to ensure an improvement in the energy efficiency of the home of at least 30%
and is provided for the purchase or construction of energy-efficient properties or for the renovation of the home in order to improve its performance.
example: Energy Fund in Emilia Romagna.
What is it? The Energy Fund is a privately held revolving fund that supports energy efficiency measures and supports expenses related to the use of renewable energies.
Beneficiaries: Small, medium and large companies operating in Emilia-Romagna can benefit from the intervention.
Eligible expenditure: Eligible projects must necessarily include investments in energy efficiency and reduction of climate-altering gases, production of energy from renewable sources exclusively for self-consumption or high-efficiency cogeneration plants.
Importi: With the Energy Fund, the Region grants subsidies of between €25,000 and €750,000, covering 100% of the project expenses.

Consumer credit

What is consumer credit?

The term "consumer credit" refers to financing that financial companies or banks may grant to allow the purchase of goods or services or the instalment of an expense, generally not referring to long-term but short-term financing.

What is the interest rate used in consumer credit?

The interest rate applied to these types of loans is much higher than that applied to mortgages.

What is the purpose of consumer credit?

The purpose of granting credit is, therefore, the financing of current household expenditure in order to support consumption, to defer payment or to pay it in instalments.

What are the tools used to access consumer credit?

The financial instruments that are used to access consumer credit are:
Credit card;
Payment in arrears or instalments;;
Personal loan: it is made with an initial payment in one instalment and the repayment will follow the amortization plan.
(The loan rarely exceeds 15000 euros);
Transfer of the fifth of the salary;
Debt consolidation.

Who can apply for consumer credit?

Any person can apply for a consumer credit, while only the person who is deemed by the lender to be able to repay the amount requested will receive the financing.

What forms of financing can be provided to a consumer?

Consumer credit includes any form of deferred payment, loan or other financial facility granted by the lender to a natural person acting for purposes outside his or her trade, business, craft or profession. Consumer credit is between EUR 200 and EUR 75 000.

Which lenders can I turn to for consumer credit?

The consumer can obtain credit by turning to:
- banks (all banks offer financing to consumers);
- specialised financial companies (they are financial intermediaries supervised by the Bank of Italy);
- Poste Italiane (which offers financing from a specialised financial company);
